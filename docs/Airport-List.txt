1. Primary Gateways (Major International Hubs)
These are the seven "Major" designated international airports that handle the vast majority of Australia's passenger and freight traffic. 
Sydney Kingsford Smith Airport (SYD) – Australia's busiest airport.
Melbourne Airport (MEL) – The primary 24/7 hub for Victoria.
Brisbane Airport (BNE) – Major gateway for Queensland and 2032 Olympics preparation.
Perth Airport (PER) – The main western gateway for international and mining traffic.
Adelaide Airport (ADL) – South Australia's primary international and domestic hub.
Cairns Airport (CNS) – Key international entry for North Queensland tourism.
Darwin International Airport (DRW) – Northern Australia's strategic international hub.
Note: Western Sydney International Airport (WSI) is scheduled to join this list upon its opening in late 2026. 
2. Secondary & Restricted International Airports
These airports have "Restricted Use" or "Non-Scheduled" international designations. While they may have permanent customs facilities, their international flights are often limited to specific routes (e.g., New Zealand, Bali) or seasonal services. 
Gold Coast Airport (OOL) – Extensive international flights to NZ and Asia.
Canberra Airport (CBR) – Serves limited international routes for the capital.
Hobart International Airport (HBA) – Primarily serves international flights to New Zealand.
Avalon Airport (AVV) – Victoria's second international gateway, with new 2026 flights to Bali.
Sunshine Coast Airport (MCY) – Seasonal international services to New Zealand and Bali.
Townsville Airport (TSV) – Limited international status, primarily regional.
Newcastle Airport (NTL) – Regional hub with limited international capabilities.
Broome International Airport (BME) – Restricted use international status.
Port Hedland International Airport (PHE) – Restricted use international status.
Toowoomba Wellcamp Airport (WTB) – Restricted use, primarily for international freight. 
3. Major Domestic & Regional Hubs
These airports are "major" in terms of domestic passenger volume and economic importance but generally lack scheduled international commercial passenger flights. 
Launceston Airport (LST) – Tasmania's second busiest airport.
Whitsunday Coast Airport (PPP) – Australia's fastest-growing airport for passenger traffic in 2025/2026.
Alice Springs Airport (ASP) – Critical regional hub for Central Australia.
Rockhampton Airport (ROK) – Major regional hub for Central Queensland.
Mackay Airport (MKY) – Key domestic hub for the mining and tourism sectors.
Ballina Byron Gateway Airport (BNK) – Significant domestic traffic for the NSW North Coast.
Hamilton Island Airport (HTI) – Major domestic tourism hub.
Karratha Airport (KTA) – Critical domestic hub for the WA resources sector.
Ayers Rock Airport (AYQ) – High-volume domestic tourism airport for Uluru.
