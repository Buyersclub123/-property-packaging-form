Reviews ‚Äì Sourcing a property to package:

Property Sourcing ‚Äì Agent Dialogue & Due Diligence

## Overview

This training outlines real-world insights from Will's sourcing experience, highlighting key steps, practical examples, and red flags to look for when sourcing property, especially in hot or unfamiliar markets.

## Section 1: Initial Market Assessment

### Where to Focus

Start with growth markets like Perth, South Australia, Brisbane, Bundaberg, Gladstone. But always remember:

If something looks too good to be true ‚Äì 8 out of 10 times, it is.

Know the market intimately. For example:

- Murray Bridge (SA): Watch for flood zones.
- Vic: Many properties are priced for auctions.
- Adelaide: Some listings underpriced‚Äîverify why.

### Red Flag Tactic

Ask yourself: "Compared to what we've bought here before, does this stack up?"

- Sellers fishing for offers? May indicate something is wrong.

# Reviews ‚Äì Sourcing a property to package:

## Section 3: Agent Conversations

### Mindset When Calling

Say less, ask more.

Use satellite view live on the call to ask sharp questions. It shows diligence and elevates your status with the agent.

### Tactics

"I'm just doing some early Due Diligence before presenting this to clients."

Call PMs in the area: Ask about street feel, demand, red flags.

Use emotional intelligence (EQ) and market logic (IQ).

## Section 4: Market Logic & Rational Investing

### Use RP Data and REA (realestate.com.au) to validate pricing

Always compare regional pricing vs metro:

"If this is $620k in a regional town, what does $620k get us 2 hours closer to a capital city?"

Example: Northam (WA)

Property: 63 Gairdner Street

Asking: $630k

Spec: 3/2/2, small block

We've built better for $500k in same suburb

‚úì Tracking your comps: Never delete prior deals (new/established)‚Äîuse for cross-checking.

üìû Section 5: Reading Between the Lines in Agent Calls

Example: 8 Bingham Cct, Thorhill (NSW)
Just listed. Agent says "top end of the range" with no buyer feedback yet.
Sat open coming ‚Üí vendor likely waiting.

Smart Approach:
Ask about:
- Lease status
- Owner intention
- Prior rent (esp. for NDIS)

Note: If agent can't confirm NDIS rent, assume standard yield.

üí° The less you talk, the more the agent fills silence. Listen carefully.

‚û§ Section 6: Watch-Outs for NDIS Properties
NDIS = Not Always

Many NDIS listings now appear due to funding cuts.

NDIS homes should be:
- Accessible
- Close to hospitals
- Not remote

‚ùå Don't buy NDIS in the middle of nowhere.

‚úÖ Ask detailed questions and confirm actual rent achieved, not just agent speculation.

## Understanding Report Structure

### Typical Sections in a Report:

#### Opening Summary

**Scope and Limitations** ‚Äì Outlines what's covered

#### Defect Summary

- Major Defects ‚Äì Safety, utility loss, or significant cost
- Minor Defects ‚Äì Less urgent, but may still be costly

#### Detailed Notes & Photos

**Overall Condition Rating** (Average, Below Average, etc.)

## How to Interpret Defects

### Major Defects

These must meet one or more of:

- Safety hazard
- High cost to rectify
- If left unresolved, will become a major issue (e.g. high moisture)

### Examples:

- Poor driveways (default to major, even if acceptable in older homes)
- High moisture readings near bathrooms
- Cracks rated Category 3‚Äì4 or showing wall deflection
- Termite damage inside property
- Significant roof space damage

### Minor Defects

Still important, may add up. Some costly repairs may still be labelled minor.

## Common Issues & How to Assess

### Cracks in Walls:

Category 0‚Äì2 (Minor hairline to <5mm) = Minor
Category 3‚Äì4 (5mm‚Äì15mm+) = Major
Deflection (walls leaning in/out) = Major

**Note:** Monitor over time; don't rely solely on width.

### Uneven Flooring:

**Older homes (timber on stumps):** Usually OK
**Modern homes (concrete slab):** Red flag
May be due to subsidence, water damage, termites

## Common Issues & How to Assess

### Moisture Readings:

**Localized (e.g. laundry wall):** Often manageable
**Widespread (multiple external walls):** Likely rising damp ‚Äì expensive

**Inspectors** Use high powered torched on ceilings to detect past leaks from observing discolouration (people often perform fresh paint to create cover-ups)

### Roofing:

Cracked/missing tiles can cause ceiling bubbling
Internal staining may be only evidence ‚Äì inspector can't always walk the roof

## Pest Inspection & Termite Risk

**Tests Conducted:**
- Moisture readings
- Timber tap testing
- Visual inspection

**Risk Factors:**
- **High moisture** = Termite attractor
- **Blocked roof/wall access** = "High Risk" by default
- **Vegetation against home** = Potential entry point
- **Firewood storage** = Flagged

## Walk-Away Red Flags

Do not proceed if any of the following are found:
- Untreated internal termite damage
- Major roof structure damage
- Widespread moisture across rooms
- Rising damp in homes <100 years old
- Extensive cracking with wall deflection

**Advice:** Always arrange termite treatment after settlement
