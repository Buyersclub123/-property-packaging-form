# Sourcing

## Reviews â€“ Sourcing a property to package:

### Property Sourcing â€“ Agent Dialogue & Due Diligence

## Overview

This training outlines real-world insights from Will's sourcing experience, highlighting key steps, practical examples, and red flags to look for when sourcing property, especially in hot or unfamiliar markets.

## Section 1: Initial Market Assessment

### Where to Focus

Start with growth markets like Perth, South Australia, Brisbane, Bundaberg, Gladstone. But always remember:

**If something looks too good to be true â€“ 8 out of 10 times, it is.**

Know the market intimately. For example:

- Murray Bridge (SA): Watch for flood zones.
- Vic: Many properties are priced for auctions.
- Adelaide: Some listings underpricedâ€”verify why.

### Red Flag Tactic

Ask yourself: "Compared to what we've bought here before, does this stack up?"

- Sellers fishing for offers? May indicate something is wrong.

---

## Section 2: Case Study Examples

### Property Looks Good on Paper...

**62 San Jacinta (WA)**
- Brick, reno'd, large block.
- Asking: mid-600s. Agent suggested "late 6s".
- **BUT...**
- Satellite view: back boundary touches a petrol station.
- Also on a main road â†’ explains price.
- âœï¸ **Lesson:** Always check satellite AND street view.

### Infrastructure Watch

**17 Savage St, Morwell (VIC)**
- Brick, solid block, water tank on rear land.
- Water tanks usually sit on council-owned high ground (reservoir land).
- Located on T-intersection, thoroughfare, opposite school.
- âš ï¸ **Investigate who owns the land behind.**

### Noise Issues

**250 Egerton Dr, Winter Valley (VIC)**
- Located behind a car wash with a dog wash.
- Even with doors closed, noise leaks through.
- Tenants dislike it â†’ must price cheap + adjust rental estimates.

---

## Section 3: Agent Conversations

### Mindset When Calling

**Say less, ask more.**

Use satellite view live on the call to ask sharp questions. It shows diligence and elevates your status with the agent.

### Tactics

"I'm just doing some early Due Diligence before presenting this to clients."

Call PMs in the area: Ask about street feel, demand, red flags.

Use emotional intelligence (EQ) and market logic (IQ).

---

## Section 4: Market Logic & Rational Investing

### Use RP Data and REA (realestate.com.au) to validate pricing

Always compare regional pricing vs metro:

"If this is $620k in a regional town, what does $620k get us 2 hours closer to a capital city?"

**Example: Northam (WA)**
- Property: 63 Gairdner Street
- Asking: $630k
- Spec: 3/2/2, small block
- We've built better for $500k in same suburb

âœ“ **Tracking your comps:** Never delete prior deals (new/established)â€”use for cross-checking.

---

## Section 5: Reading Between the Lines in Agent Calls

**Example: 8 Bingham Cct, Thorhill (NSW)**
- Just listed. Agent says "top end of the range" with no buyer feedback yet.
- Sat open coming â†’ vendor likely waiting.

**Smart Approach:**
Ask about:
- Lease status
- Owner intention
- Prior rent (esp. for NDIS)

**Note:** If agent can't confirm NDIS rent, assume standard yield.

ðŸ’¡ **The less you talk, the more the agent fills silence. Listen carefully.**

---

## Section 6: Watch-Outs for NDIS Properties

**NDIS = Not Always**

Many NDIS listings now appear due to funding cuts.

**NDIS homes should be:**
- Accessible
- Close to hospitals
- Not remote

âŒ **Don't buy NDIS in the middle of nowhere.**

âœ… **Ask detailed questions and confirm actual rent achieved, not just agent speculation.**

---

## Final Takeaways

- Always do a satellite + street view check.
- Ask: "What explains this price?"
- Don't rush. Red flags? Walk away.
- Use past Buyers Club purchases as your benchmark.
- Use EQ + IQ: emotional intelligence + sharp due diligence.
- Keep agent calls strategic. Less talk = more insights.

# RP Data CMA Report Criteria

## Reviews â€“ RP Data CMA Criteria

### 1. Property Filters

- **Property Type:** Comparable (add "Unit" for Duplex / Dual)
- **Sold within:** 6 months
- **Radius:** 2 km (extended incrementally first if no comps, over 5km for regional properties only)
- **Beds:** Comparable (extended if no comps)
- **Baths:** Comparable (extended if no comps)
- **Car:** Comparable (extended if no comps)
- **Land size:** Set 50 sq m. above initially & incrementally increase as needed.

Always compare floor plans with comps if available.

Look for opportunities i.e. additional beds and highlight to BA notes at top of email review

---

### 2. Comparable Selection

**Required:** If no comps, ok to add:
- 3x Sold / Rented
- 1x Superior
- 3x On-Market
- 1x Inferior

---

### 3. DUAL KEY PROPERTY CHALLENGES

- Often no exact comps available
- Valuers tend to ignore layout (dual key) and focus on internal floor space
- Thus, final bank valuation may fall short â€” prepare to justify through replacement cost or investment potential

---

### 4. RENTAL CMA GUIDANCE

**Key Points:**
- Beds are king, land size has minimal impact on rent
- If a rent seems off, explain the outlier (seasonality, road frontage, etc.)
- Don't force a false rent range â€” show the range naturally through superior/inferior examples
- Vacant: treat rent cautiously
- Be confident in your appraisal â€” don't let poor PMs drag down the pricing logic

**Remember Comments for BA as required for:**
- Superior/inferior comps explained
- RP Data errors checked
- Justify outliers for sales / rental
- Clarify why a certain price point is valid despite fewer sales
- Opportunities i.e. extra bed by repurposing another room

---

### Key Elements for Comparability

- Title type (e.g. Torrens)
- Construction type
- Land size
- Build/floor size
- Spec level (fixtures & finishes)
- Internal layout (especially for dual key)

This aligns with valuer methodology â€” you're reverse-engineering a valuer's logic.

**If nothing comparable â€“ extend radius first before widening other criteria**

**Dual-key properties â€“ caution â€“ if no direct dual key comps exist, use floor size and replacement value instead. Ok to add unit to property type.**

**Duplex â€“ Comparable single properties can be used. Ok to add unit to property type.**

# Purchase Price

## Reviews â€“ Purchase Price: Asking, Comparable & Acquisition Range

### Asking: â€“ how the property is positioned in the market

**Definitions:**

- **On-market** â€“
  - The property is on market with ads on REA, Domain etc

- **Off-market** â€“
  - The property is not advertised on REA, Domain etc AND there is no plan by the agent to launch an online campaign

- **Pre-launch opportunity** â€“
  - The property is not advertised on REA, Domain etc BUT there is a plan by the agent to launch an online campaign in the future

- **Coming soon** â€“
  - We see this occasionally where the agents try to create awareness of a property coming to market soon or to gather price range interest

### Asking + Price range info:

- **On-market** â€“
  - Detail range as advertised
  - i.e. "Asking: On-market - $450,000 - $520,000"

- **Off-market** â€“
  - Inflate the range given by agent, ensuring it can be supported by comparable

- **Pre-launch opportunity** â€“
  - Detail range as expected to be advertised
  - i.e. "Asking: Pre-launch opportunity - $450,000 - $520,000"

- **Coming soon** â€“
  - Detail range as advertised

### Examples:

**Purchase Price**
- Asking: On-market - $475,000 - $480,000
- Comparable Sales: We have seen comparable properties trade into the high 400k's to low 500k's
- Accepted Acquisition Target: $450,000 - $470,000

## Comparable Sales & Acquisition Range

The comparable sales information is sourced via the online CMA reports from **CoreLogic - RP Data website**

### Wording:

It is essential we use common language associated with real estate, such as:

**Numeral's:**
When providing comparable ranges, we can say simplify by using a shortened number. See below highlights:
"Comparable properties are currently trading in the vicinity of mid 400k's to early 500k's"

**Note:** No Dollar Sign ($) is used | We use a shortened number | Utilising "k's" to indicate thousands

**Non or few true comparable properties:**
If the property type not often sold in the suburb, we can use wording such as:
"Similar properties are seldom traded. However, we have seen single family 3 bed homes selling in mid to high 300k's"

**Acquisition Range:** Must be a target that the property can be secured for, but also one that demonstrates value.

## How to Interpret Defects

### Major Defects

These must meet one or more of:
- Safety hazard
- High cost to rectify
- If left unresolved, will become a major issue (e.g. high moisture)

### Examples:

- Poor driveways (default to major, even if acceptable in older homes)
- High moisture readings near bathrooms
- Cracks rated Category 3â€“4 or showing wall deflection
- Termite damage inside property
- Significant roof space damage

### Minor Defects

Still important, may add up. Some costly repairs may still be labelled minor.

---

## Common Issues & How to Assess

### Cracks in Walls:

- **Category 0â€“2** (Minor hairline to <5mm) = Minor
- **Category 3â€“4** (5mmâ€“15mm+) = Major
- **Deflection** (walls leaning in/out) = Major

**Note:** Monitor over time; don't rely solely on width.

### Uneven Flooring:

- **Older homes (timber on stumps):** Usually OK
- **Modern homes (concrete slab):** Red flag
- May be due to subsidence, water damage, termites

---

## Common Issues & How to Assess

### Moisture Readings:

- **Localized (e.g. laundry wall):** Often manageable
- **Widespread (multiple external walls):** Likely rising damp â€“ expensive

**Inspectors** Use high powered torched on ceilings to detect past leaks from observing discolouration (people often perform fresh paint to create cover-ups)

### Roofing:

Cracked/missing tiles can cause ceiling bubbling
Internal staining may be only evidence â€“ inspector can't always walk the roof

---

## Pest Inspection & Termite Risk

**Tests Conducted:**
- Moisture readings
- Timber tap testing
- Visual inspection

**Risk Factors:**
- **High moisture** = Termite attractor
- **Blocked roof/wall access** = "High Risk" by default
- **Vegetation against home** = Potential entry point
- **Firewood storage** = Flagged

**Advice:** Always arrange termite treatment after settlement

---

## Walk-Away Red Flags

Do not proceed if any of the following are found:
- Untreated internal termite damage
- Major roof structure damage
- Widespread moisture across rooms
- Rising damp in homes <100 years old
- Extensive cracking with wall deflection
